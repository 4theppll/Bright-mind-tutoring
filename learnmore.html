Our Founding

Bright Minds Tutoring was founded in 2025 by Salahdiin Adan, a student who recognized the growing educational disparities in our community. What began as a small initiative to help a few classmates quickly grew into a full-fledged tutoring organization serving students across Colorado.

Our mission is simple: to provide high-quality, personalized tutoring completely free of charge to students who need it most. We believe that every student deserves access to educational support, regardless of their family's financial situation.

"Education is the great equalizer. By providing free tutoring, we're helping level the playing field for students who might otherwise fall behind."

— Salahdiin Ahmed Adan, Founder

Why We Do It

The COVID-19 pandemic exacerbated existing educational inequalities, with many students falling significantly behind in core subjects like math and science. We saw firsthand how these learning gaps affected our peer's confidence and future opportunities.

Our volunteer tutors are passionate about reversing this trend. We focus not just on academic improvement, but also on rebuilding students' confidence in their abilities. Many of our tutors come from similar backgrounds as the students we serve, allowing us to create meaningful connections and serve as relatable role models.

Our Journey

Key moments in our organization's growth

Founding Bright Minds

July 2025

Salahdiin Adan officially establishes Bright Minds Tutoring after seeing the need for free academic support among his peers. The organization begins with just 1 volunteer tutors helping a handful of students.

Meeting Mayor Coffman

August 2025

Bright Minds is invited to meet with Aurora Mayor Mike Coffman to discuss educational initiatives in the community. The meeting leads to increased visibility and support for our programs.

Westword Feature

August 2025

Bright Minds is featured in Westword magazine's education issue, highlighting our innovative approach to peer tutoring and community-based learning support.

Denver7 News Interview

September 2025

Our founder is interviewed on Denver7 News about the impact of free tutoring services on student achievement. The segment leads to a surge in volunteer applications and student sign-ups.
